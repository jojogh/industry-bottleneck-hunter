# Chokepoint Criteria

Use this reference during **Phase 3: Chokepoint Scan**.

## The Strait-of-Hormuz Link

A chokepoint is a narrow value-chain node that is small in output but irreplaceable in systemic importance.

| Trait | Meaning in Supply-Chain Context |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| **Narrow channel** | Few or single suppliers globally |
| **High throughput dependency** | Large downstream value depends on this node |
| **No viable alternative route** | Switching cost or substitution is prohibitively expensive |
| **Easy to control** | Dominant player can restrict supply or raise prices |
| **Disproportionate impact** | A small disruption cascades into large downstream losses |

## Segment Screening Criteria

For each value-chain link, score 1–5 on each criterion. Links scoring ≥18/25 are candidate chokepoints.

| Criterion | Question | High-Score Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Concentration Ratio** | Do the top 3 players control >70% global supply? | Top-3 share ≥70% |
| **Technology Barrier** | Does production require >5 years of R&D or proprietary know-how? | Deep, hard-to-replicate process IP |
| **Substitution Difficulty** | Would switching suppliers require redesigning downstream products? | Product or process redesign needed |
| **Capacity Inelasticity** | Can supply scale quickly in response to demand? | No — long lead times, scarce equipment, limited feedstock |
| **Value Capture** | Does this node capture high margin despite small final-product cost share? | High gross margin relative to chain value |

## Company Screening Criteria

Within each chokepoint segment, rank candidate companies on:

| Criterion | Target Profile |
|---|---|
| **Output Scale** | Annual revenue or volume is small relative to downstream market size (prefer <5% of chain value) |
| **Technology Monopoly** | Holds core patents, trade secrets, or process IP that competitors cannot replicate within 3–5 years |
| **Customer Lock-in** | Long qualification cycles, design integration, or sole-source contracts |
| **Pricing Power** | Demonstrated ability to raise prices without significant volume loss |
| **Defensibility** | Network effects, learning curves, or regulatory barriers protect the position |
